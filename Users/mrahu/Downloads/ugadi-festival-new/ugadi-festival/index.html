The story of creation begins…

Chapter I

The Cosmic Ocean

In the infinite cosmic ocean of milk, Lord Vishnu sleeps in divine slumber upon the great serpent Adishesha, the thousand-headed guardian of the cosmos.

From His navel, a magnificent golden lotus blooms, rising through the primordial waters towards the infinite sky.

Brahma awakens and begins the creation of the universe — the Kalpa Day

Chapter II

The Vedas Are Stolen

Before Brahma fully awakens, the fearsome Asura Hayagrīva — the horse-headed demon — steals the sacred Vedas from Brahma's grasp.

Without the Vedas, the blueprints of existence itself, the creation of the universe becomes impossible.

⚠

Creation is at risk. The cosmic order hangs in the balance. Without divine knowledge, the universe cannot be born.

Chapter III

Matsya Avatar — The Cosmic Rescue

Lord Vishnu transforms into Matsya — the magnificent golden fish — His first avatar among the Dashavatara.

He dives deep into the infinite cosmic ocean, pursuing the demon Hayagrīva through the primordial waters. A fierce battle ensues in the depths of creation.

⚔️ After a cosmic battle, Matsya defeats Hayagrīva and retrieves the sacred Vedas, returning them to Brahma.

Chapter IV

Brahma Begins The Creation

With the sacred Vedas restored, Lord Brahma begins the magnificent act of creation — shaping the cosmos, the stars, the earth, all living beings.

The divine knowledge flows from the Vedas like rivers of light, forming the fabric of reality itself.

The Cosmic Clock

Just as Monday returns after Sunday, the universe does not move in a straight line. It moves in a Great Circle.

Time (Kaala) has no absolute beginning or end, only endless cycles of blooming and folding. Ugadi is not just a New Year—it is the very moment this cosmic wheel completes a turn and starts its breathtaking journey all over again.

⏳ A day for Brahma. A lifetime for the Universe.

Treta Yuga

Rama's Coronation

On the auspicious day of Ugadi, with the Sun and Moon in perfect cosmic alignment of the lunisolar cycle, Lord Rama was crowned king of Ayodhya.

The heavens rejoiced. Dharma was reestablished. A golden age of righteousness dawned.

Krishna's Departure

In a quiet forest, the hunter Jara, mistaking Krishna's feet for a deer's, releases his fateful arrow…

The final moments of Dvapara Yuga unfold…

This fateful arrow marks the end of Dvapara Yuga and the dawn of Kali Yuga — the current age of humanity.

Chapter VI

The Meaning of Ugadi

युग Yuga Age / Era
+
आदि Ādi Beginning
=
युगादि Yugādi The Beginning of a New Age

Ugadi celebrates the commencement of a new cosmic cycle — a moment when time itself is renewed. It is the first day of the Hindu lunisolar calendar, marking the start of the new year in the Deccan region of India.

Just as Brahma began creation anew, so too does every being get a chance to begin again — with hope, with resolve, with the sweetness and bitterness that life offers.

Chapter VII

Nature's Renewal

After the rains recede, the earth awakens. New leaves unfurl on ancient trees, shimmering in tender green — a sign that life begins again.

The mango trees burst into bloom — their pale golden flowers promising the sweetness of fruit to come. Nature herself celebrates Ugadi.

Chapter VIII
